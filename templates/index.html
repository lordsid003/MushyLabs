Mushrooms have Anti-Oxidant, Anti-Bacterial, Anti-Tumor and neuroprotective effects.

Chemistry tells us that mushrooms are composed of various substances that have different structures, functions, and effects.

  • Phenolic compounds: These are organic molecules that contain one or more hydroxyl groups attached to a benzene ring.
  • Fatty acids and lipids: These are organic molecules that consist of a hydrocarbon chain and a carboxyl group. They are important components of cell membranes and energy sources.
  • Triterpenoids and sterols: These are lipids that have a carbon skeleton of four or more fused rings.

Mushrooms: A Superfood for Health

Nutritional analysis of mushrooms:

  • They are the only natural source of Vitamin D in the produce aisle, and some varieties can increase their vitamin D content by exposure to ultraviolet light.
  • They are also rich in B vitamins, such as riboflavin, niacin, and folate, which help to provide energy and support metabolism, nervous system, and blood cell formation.
  • Mushrooms are low in calories, fat, and carbs, but high in protein and fiber, making them a great choice for weight management and digestive health.
